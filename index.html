Literature Review

“How to measure food loss and waste? A material flow analysis application” published by V. Amicarelli, C. Bux and G. Lagioia emphasizes on adapting material flow analysis as a method to measure food loss and waste [13]. The main application of material flow analysis in manufacturing is to measure the waste produced at each step of the workflow-defined processes. In order to demonstrate how material flow analysis can be used to measure or quantify waste, the research used one consumption-ready food and one ingredient. In the research paper “Analysis of post-harvest losses: An Internet of Things and machine learning approach” done by Computer Engineering Department, PICT, Pune, Maharashtra, India has developed a machine learning algorithm which predicts which stock would degrade first [16]. It provides information to the warehouse managers to come up with a dispatch sequence rather than following the first in first out method. Further it discusses about the parameters which affect the food degradation. It has been found that the amount of storage loss is determined by factors such as the number of days the stock has been held, warehouse temperature and humidity, and harvesting time deviation from the optimal harvesting period (in number of days) [16].

“Minimization of Food Waste in Retail Sector using Time-Series Analysis and Object Detection Algorithm” by H. Agarwal, B. Ahir, P. Bide, S. Jain and H. Barot [1], discusses on a solution to reduce food wastage through the use of an inventory prediction system, smart dustbins and redistribution. The inventory prediction system aims to forecast the inventory needed by the restaurant at a given time.

Methodology

Focusing on reducing food loss and waste from a single process or treating the processes as independent has made existing solutions or research less effective and efficient in addressing the core problem addressed because every process in the supply chain is interconnected and important for decision-making (consumption data is essential to plan purchases for the following day). This is what distinguishes the research conducted by the author and co-authors as being distinctive and successful because it focuses on all of the supply chain processes identified and ensures that transparency is affirmed to support collaborative data flow and combined data analysis to mitigate the core problem through informed decisions. Each defined process also has distinctive features or solutions. The research addresses the two main problems for hotels, which are transparency (not knowing what happens in other departments in the food supply chain) and not knowing the processes that cause excessive food loss and waste. There is a comparable commercial product named Winnow Solution that has been explored for the regional, Asian Pacific, and international markets. The product's flaw is that it ignores other activities and only takes manufacturing and consumption processes into account. The distribution, purchasing, storage, production, services, and consuming processes along the food supply chain are the focus of the proposed solution. Identifying data collection points, setting up data capture apps, and organizing the flow of captured data among all other components are the key goals of each process. Additionally, each component has a specific function for process optimization or enhancement to reduce food loss and waste, as shown in the diagram below. The proposed solution will be commercialized as Foodligence. The features include a "Demand Predictor" that uses order data and past purchases data to forecast the minimum amount of stock needed to meet daily operational demand and reduce purchasing process losses, a "Expiry Predictor with Freshness Index" that provides the best before date for perishable ingredients and food items to assure all the purchased goods are used effectively to prevent any food losses or waste, and a platform that allows subscribers to “Donate” the excess to needy. In order to prevent food wastage at the consumption level, "Portion Size Predictor" focuses on determining the appropriate portion size for a customer depending on the criteria entered. Furthermore, “Collaborative Data Flow and Analytics” concentrate on all processes to provides improved transparency of processes for effective decision making to control food loss and waste, making recommendations for customers and quantifying food loss and waste using defined analytical measures.

Item-based recommendation was selected as the strongest algorithm for making recommendations on menu choices. The author has pinned with item-based because user-based has considerable restrictions, as stated in the methodologies chapter. Additionally, from a business perspective, hotels and restaurants want their customers to be exposed to more and more products instead of sticking to a limited selection when looking at their sales. Item-based ensures that customers are exposed to more food items while user-based only exposes them to a limited range.
